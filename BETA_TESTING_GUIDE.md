# 🏰 Arambh Beta Testing Guide

Welcome to the Arambh Beta! You are among the first 20 adventurers to explore PyQuest — a fantasy RPG where you learn Python by conquering regions, battling bosses, and earning XP.

This guide will walk you through everything you need to know.

---

## 1. How to Register

1. Open the Arambh URL shared with you by the founder.
2. Click **"Join Guild"** in the top-right corner (or on the landing page).
3. Fill in three fields:
   - **Username** — Your adventurer name (e.g., `shadow_coder`). This will appear on the Leaderboard.
   - **Email** — A valid email address.
   - **Password** — Choose a strong password.
4. Click **"Create Character"**.
5. You will be automatically logged in and redirected to your Dashboard.

> **Tip:** Your username is permanent and visible to other players. Choose wisely!

## 2. How to Start Learning

### Step 1: Open the World Map
Click **"World Map"** in the navigation bar. You'll see 12 fantasy regions — each one teaching a Python concept.

### Step 2: Enter Your First Region
Your first available region is **Variables Forest** (Region 1). Click on it to open the regional lesson list.

### Step 3: Start a Lesson
Click on any unlocked lesson. Each lesson has:
- **A hook** — Why this concept matters.
- **Mental model** — Key ideas to hold in your mind.
- **Step Debugger** — Walk through real Python code line by line. Click "Run Line" to advance.
- **Live Memory Panel** — Watch variables appear and change in real-time as you step through.

### Step 4: Complete the Lesson
When you reach the final step, click **"Complete +50 XP"** to earn experience points and unlock the next lesson.

### Step 5: Practice in Training Grounds
After a lesson, click **"Practice in Training Ground"** or navigate to the Training Ground from the nav bar. Answer multiple-choice challenges at Easy, Medium, and Hard difficulty levels.

### Step 6: Defeat the Region Boss
Once you complete all 4 lessons in a region, the **Boss Challenge** unlocks. This is a multi-stage coding battle that tests everything you learned. Defeat the boss to unlock the next region.

## 3. How to Report Bugs

We rely on your reports to improve Arambh before public launch.

### Using the Bug Report Widget
1. Look for the **"Report Bug"** button floating in the bottom-right corner of every page.
2. Click it. A form will appear.
3. Select a category:
   - **Technical Bug** — Something broken (crash, error, blank screen).
   - **Content Issue** — Typo, wrong explanation, incorrect answer.
   - **UI / UX Suggestion** — Layout problems, confusing navigation.
   - **Other / Feedback** — General thoughts or ideas.
4. Describe the issue in detail. Include:
   - What you were doing.
   - What you expected to happen.
   - What actually happened.
5. Click **"Submit Report"**. Your browser info and current page URL are automatically captured.

### Using the Lesson Feedback Widget
At the bottom of every lesson, you'll see:
> **"Was this lesson helpful?"** 👍 Yes / 👎 No

Please click one every time. This helps us identify which lessons need improvement.

## 4. How to Use the AI Mentor

The AI Mentor (called **"Oracle's Guide"**) is your personal Socratic tutor available inside every lesson.

### How to Summon
1. While on any lesson page, look for the **"Summon Mentor"** button (bottom-right, with a ✨ sparkle icon).
2. Click it. A side panel slides open.

### What You Can Ask
- "Explain this code in simple terms."
- "Give me a hint about what's happening on this line."
- "Can you suggest an analogy for this concept?"
- "What are common mistakes beginners make here?"

### Important Rules
- The Mentor uses **Socratic questioning** — it won't give you the direct answer, but will guide you to discover it yourself.
- It can see your current lesson context and code state.
- Pre-built suggestion chips are provided for quick prompts.

### Tips for Best Results
- Be specific: "Why does `x = 5` store the number 5?" is better than "I don't get it."
- Use it when stuck — not as a shortcut. The learning happens in the struggle.

## 5. How to Use the Memory Vault

The **Memory Vault** is a spaced repetition system that helps you retain what you've learned long-term.

### How It Works
1. After you complete lessons and explore the **Library**, concepts are added to your review queue.
2. Navigate to **"Memory Vault"** in the top navigation bar.
3. If you have due reviews, you'll see a quiz question about a concept you've previously studied.
4. Answer the question.
5. Rate your confidence:
   - **Easy** (5) — You knew it instantly.
   - **Good** (4) — You remembered after thinking.
   - **Hard** (3) — You struggled but got it.
   - **Forgot** (1) — You didn't remember.
6. Based on your rating, the system schedules the next review using the SM-2 algorithm. Concepts you forget appear sooner; ones you know well appear later.

### When to Use It
- **Daily** — Ideally once per day. Even 5 minutes helps.
- After completing a new region — to consolidate what you just learned.

### Empty Vault
If you see **"Memory Vault Clear"**, it means:
- You have no reviews due today, OR
- You haven't studied enough concepts yet. Complete more Library readings to populate it.
